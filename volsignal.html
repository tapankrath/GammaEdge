How It Works

This tool compares implied volatility (IV) — the market's expected move priced into options — against realized volatility (RV), the actual movement that occurred. The spread between them determines whether options are cheap or expensive relative to real risk.

When IV > RV, selling volatility has a statistical edge. When RV > IV, buying volatility does. IV is sourced from VIX/VXN via Finnhub; RV is calculated from 30-day price history via Twelve Data.
